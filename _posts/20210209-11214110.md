<p class="picture" style="font-size: 13px; font-family: Times New Roman; color: #999; margin-top: 3px">Yoshiro Mori (C), president of Tokyo 2020 Organizing Committee, speaks during a joint press conference between the International Olympic Committee (IOC) and Tokyo 2020 on Sept. 25, 2020.Photo:Xinhua</p>Tokyo Olympics chief Yoshiro Mori apologized but refused to resign on Thursday after making sexist remarks that prompted outrage in Japan.<br><br>Mori's comments, in which he said women speak too much in meetings, are the latest headache for organizers already battling public disquiet about the pandemic-postponed Games.<br><br>The 83-year-old former prime minister triggered further criticism when he admitted "I don't speak to women much" in a hastily organized press conference called to make his apology.<br><br>And while Mori said his initial remarks were "inappropriate" he became defensive when questioned, insisting he had heard complaints that women speak at length.<br><br>Mori is reported to have said Wednesday that "board of directors meetings with many women take a lot of time," according to the Asahi Shimbun daily.<br><br>"When you increase the number of female executive members, if their speaking time isn't restricted to a certain extent, they have difficulty finishing, which is annoying," he was quoted as saying.<br><br>Mori, who is known for his gaffes, did not dispute the report and told reporters that he wanted to "retract" his sexist comments.<br><br>"What I said... went against the spirit of the Olympics and Paralympics, and I recognize that it was inappropriate," he said.<br><br>But he added that he was "not thinking about resigning" and insisted he was responding to comments he had heard about women speaking for too long.<br><br>The comments sparked a furious reaction in Japan, with the phrases "enough already," "misogyny" and "we demand Yoshiro Mori resigns" all trending on Twitter.<br><br>AFP <br>
