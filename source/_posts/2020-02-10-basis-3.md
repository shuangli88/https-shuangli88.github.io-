Photo: VCG</p><br />The Chilean government received a second shipment of nearly 2 million vaccines developed by Chinese firm Sinovac Biotech on Sunday, which will be used to begin the mass vaccination in Chile, scheduled to start on Wednesday.<br /><br />Chilean Deputy Health Minister Paula Daza said at a press conference that the arrival of the two shipments of Sinovac vaccine CoronaVac will allow for the vaccination of about 4 million people in February.<br /><br />"There is work and logistics that are being done for vaccinations in the field with municipality representatives," she said.<br /><br />Chilean President Sebastian Pinera wrote on Twitter after the arrival of the second shipment of the Chinese vaccine that mass vaccination will begin on Wednesday, starting with high-risk groups.<br /><br />Chile on Thursday received a shipment of nearly 2 million Sinovac vaccines as the South American country is preparing for mass vaccination campaign against COVID-19.<br /><br />According to the Ministry of Health, Chile registered another 4,209 cases of COVID-19 on Sunday, bringing the total to 727,109, with 18,452 deaths.<br /><br /> <br>
