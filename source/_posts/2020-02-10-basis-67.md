Photo: VCG</p>Saudi Arabia on Thursday shut down entertainment centers including cinemas and suspended dine-in services at restaurants, tightening coronavirus restrictions amid a spike in infections.<br><br>The kingdom suspended "all events and parties" including weddings in banquet halls or hotels for 30 days, the interior ministry said in a statement released by the official Saudi Press Agency.<br><br>It also halted "all entertainment activities" including cinemas and gaming parlours and suspended dine-in services in all restaurants and cafes for 10 days, it added, warning that the suspension could be extended.<br><br>The measures, which will be implemented from 10 pm Thursday (1900 GMT) are aimed at "preventing the outbreak of a second wave" of the pandemic, the ministry said.<br><br>The announcement comes after Health Minister Tawfiq al-Rabiah warned on Sunday that new coronavirus restrictions could be imposed if citizens and residents do not comply with health restrictions.<br><br>On Wednesday, Saudi Arabia suspended entry from 20 countries, ranging from some neighboring states to the US, in a bid to curb a jump in infections.<br><br>Saudi Arabia has reported nearly 369,000 coronavirus cases and almost 6,400 deaths, the highest among Gulf Arab states.<br><br>Daily infections dipped below 100 in early January, from a peak of nearly 5,000 last June.<br><br>However, new daily COVID-19 infections have tripled since then, with 306 cases reported by the health ministry on Wednesday.<br><br>AFP <br>
