<p class="picture" style="font-size: 13px; font-family: Times New Roman; margin-top: 3px; color: #999">File photo:Xinhua</p><br />Cambodian Prime Minister Samdech Techo Hun Sen said on Friday that an inoculation drive against COVID-19 will start from Feb 10 after the first batch of China-donated Sinopharm vaccines is due to arrive in the kingdom on Sunday.<br /><br />"At 8:30 am (local time) on Feb 10, 2021, vaccinations will officially start at the Calmette Hospital," he wrote on his official Facebook page.<br /><br />"This vaccination process will be live broadcast on TVs and social media platforms and after receiving the first shot of the vaccine, I will hold a press conference at the Calmette Hospital," he added.<br /><br />
<img src="https://00and.com/images/2021-02-09-22dubd30yde.jpg" />
<p class="picture" style="font-size: 13px; font-family: Times New Roman; margin-top: 3px; color: #999">Cambodian medical staff stand near refrigeration vehicles at the Preah Ket Mealea Hospital in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, Feb. 3, 2021. A team of Chinese vaccine specialists have provided training to Cambodian medical staff on how to use the anti-COVID-19 vaccine ahead of its arrival.Photo:Xinhua</p><br />According to the prime minister, the vaccines will be provided free-of-charge to people who are at a high risk of getting infections such as medics, teachers, bodyguards, armed forces, tuk-tuk and taxi drivers, and garbage collectors, among others.<br /><br />He added that the Southeast Asian nation has planned to inoculate between 10 and 13 million people, or about 80 percent of the population.<br /><br />The Ministry of Health on Thursday authorized the emergency use of Sinopharm COVID-19 vaccine, saying that "the vaccine is used safely in China and other countries."<br /><br />Cambodia has so far registered a total of 470 confirmed COVID-19 cases, with zero deaths and 452 recoveries, according to the Ministry of Health.<br /><br /> <br>
